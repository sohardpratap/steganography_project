What is Steganography?

Steganography is the practice of hiding a secret message inside another medium, such as an image or video, in such a way that the presence of the message is not apparent. The word steganography is derived from the Greek words “steganos” (meaning hidden or covered) and “graphein” (meaning to write).

How It Works

In digital steganography, data is hidden by manipulating the pixels of an image. For example, you can embed a secret message by modifying the least significant bit (LSB) of the RGB values of an image. Because these slight changes are imperceptible to the human eye, the image looks identical to the original, but it contains hidden information.

Steps Involved in Steganography:

  • Select an image to hide the message in.
  • Convert the message to binary form.
  • Modify the image’s pixel data to store the message.
  • Retrieve the message by decoding the pixel data.

Applications of Steganography:

  • Secure communication
  • Watermarking digital content
  • Digital forensics
  • Copyright protection
